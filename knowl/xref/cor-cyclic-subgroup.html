Corollary 2.15. Elements of \(\igen x\).

For an element \(x\) of \(G\text{,}\) the elements of \(\langle x \rangle\) can be described as: \(\langle x\rangle=\{x^j \mid j\in \mathbb{Z}\}\text{.}\)

Proof.

By LemmaÂ 2.3, the group \(G\) has the following elements \(G=\{x^i \mid i\in \mathbb{Z}\}\text{.}\)
First, we show \(|G|\geq n\text{.}\) I claim each of the elements \(x^0, x^1, \dots, x^{n-1}\) are distinct. Indeed, if \(0\leq i<j<n\) and \(x^i=x^j\) then \(x^{j-i}=e\) and \(1\leq j-i<n\text{,}\) contradicting the minimality of the order of \(x\text{.}\)
Next, we show \(|G|\leq n\text{.}\) I claim \(G\subseteq\{x^0, x^1, \dots, x^{n-1}\}\) (this implies \(G=\{e,x,\ldots,x^{n-1}\}\)). Indeed, for any \(m\in \mathbb{Z}\) [cross-reference to target(s) "thm-division-algorithm" missing or not unique] by \(n\) yields integers \(q,r\) with \(0\leq r\leq n-1\) such that \(m=nq+r\text{.}\) Then \(x^m=x^{nq+r}=(x^n)^qx^r=x^r\in\{x^0, x^1, \dots, x^{n-1}\}\text{.}\)
