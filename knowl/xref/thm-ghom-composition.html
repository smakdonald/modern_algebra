Theorem 1.65. Compositions of Homomorphisms.

If \(\varphi:G\to H\) and \(\phi:H\to K\) are group homomorphisms, the composition \(\phi\circ\varphi:G\to K\) is a group homomorphism.

Proof.

Suppose \(\varphi:G\to H\) and \(\phi:H\to K\) be group homomorphisms, and let \(x,y\in G\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \phi\circ\varphi(xy) &= \phi\circ\left(\varphi(x)\varphi(y)\right) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-ghom.html}{\text{Definition 1.59}} \text{ applied to } \varphi \\ &= \phi\left(\varphi(x)\varphi(y)\right) && [cross-reference to target(s) "def-composition" missing or not unique] \\ &= \phi\left(\varphi(x)\right)\phi\left(\varphi(y)\right) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-ghom.html}{\text{Definition 1.59}} \text{ applied to } \phi\\ &= \left(\phi\circ\varphi(x)\right)\left(\phi\circ\varphi(y)\right) && [cross-reference to target(s) "def-composition" missing or not unique] \end{align*}
Thus \(\phi\circ\varphi:G\to K\) is a group homomorphism.
