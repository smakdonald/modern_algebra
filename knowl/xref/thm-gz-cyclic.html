Theorem 3.40. Cyclic Center Quotients.

Let \(G\) be a group with center \(Z(G)\text{.}\) If \(G/Z(G)\) is cyclic, then \(G\) is abelian.

Proof.

Let \(G\) be a group with center \(Z(G)\text{,}\) and suppose \(G/Z(G)\) is cyclic. Let \(x,y\in G\text{.}\) If either \(x\) or \(y\) are in the center of \(G\text{,}\) then \(xy=yx\text{,}\) as desired. Suppose then neither \(x\) nor \(y\) are in the center of \(G\text{.}\) Thus \(xZ(G)\) and \(yZ(G)\) are non-identity cosets in \(G/Z(G)\text{.}\) As \(G/Z(G)\) is cyclic, there exists some \(z\in G\) such that \(G/Z(G)=\{zZ(G)^j|j\in\Z\}\text{.}\) Thus there exist \(m,n\in\Z\) such that \(xZ(G) = zZ(G)^m\) and \(yZ(G) = zZ(G)^n\text{,}\) respectively.
