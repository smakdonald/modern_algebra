Lemma 5.15. Largest Normal Subgroup.

Let \(H\) be a subgroup of a group \(G\text{,}\) and let \(G\) act on the set \(G/H\) of left cosets by left multiplication \(g \cdot (g'H) = (gg')H\text{.}\) Let \(\rho: G\to \Perm(G/H)\) denote the permutation representation associated to this action. Then
  1. the action described above is transitive
  2. \(\displaystyle \ker(\rho) = \bigcap_{g \in G} gHg^{-1}\)
  3. the subgroup described in (2) is the largest (with respect to containment) normal subgroup of \(G\) contained in \(H\text{.}\)
