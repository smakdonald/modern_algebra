Theorem 2.21. Cyclic Groups are Abelian.

Every cyclic group is abelian.

Proof.

Suppose \(G\) is a cylic group, and let \(g,h\in G\text{.}\) As \(G\) is cyclic, \(G=\igen{x}\) for some \(x\in G\text{.}\) By CorollaryÂ 2.15, \(G=\{x^j|j\in\Z}\text{.}\) Thus \(g=x^m\) and \(h=x^n\) for some \(m,n\in\Z\text{.}\) Observe:
\begin{equation*} gh = x^mx^n = x^{m+n} = x^nx^m = hg. \end{equation*}
Hence \(G\) is abelian.
