Theorem 1.63. Homomorphism Preservations.

If \(\varphi: G \to H\) is a homomorphism of groups, then the following hold:
  1. \(\displaystyle \varphi(e_G) = e_H\)
  2. \(\varphi(x^{-1}) = \varphi(x)^{-1}\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(\varphi: G \to H\) be a homomorphism of groups.
  1. Observe:
    \begin{align*} \varphi(e_G) &= \varphi(e_Ge_G)\\ &= \varphi(e_G)\varphi(e_G) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-ghom.html}{\text{Definition 1.59}}\\ \varphi(e_G)\varphi(e_G)\inv &= \varphi(e_G)\varphi(e_G)\varphi(e_G)\inv &&\text{multiply by }\varphi(e_G)\inv\\ e_H &= \varphi(e_G) \end{align*}
  2. Let \(x\in G\text{.}\) I claim that \(\varphi(x\inv)\) is the inverse of \(\varphi(x)\) in \(H\text{:}\)
    \begin{align*} \varphi(x)\varphi(x\inv) &= \varphi(xx\inv) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-ghom.html}{\text{Definition 1.59}}\\ &= \varphi(e_G)\\ &= e_H && \knowl{./knowl/xref/gp-ident-pres.html}{\text{Part 1}} \end{align*}
    Thus \(\varphi(x\inv)\) and \(\varphi(x)\inv\) are both inverses of \(\varphi(x)\) in \(H\text{,}\) and so \(\varphi(x^{-1}) = \varphi(x)\inv\text{,}\) since inverses are unique.
