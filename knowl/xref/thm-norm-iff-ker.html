Theorem 3.39. Normal Subgroup iff Kernel.

A subgroup \(N\) of a group \(G\) is normal in \(G\) if and only if \(N\) is the kernel of a homomorphism with domain \(G\text{.}\)

Proof.

First, suppose \(K\nsg G\text{.}\) Let \(H=G/K\) (which is a group since \(K\) is normal in \(G\)) and define \(\varphi: G\to G/K\) such that \(\varphi(g)=gK\text{.}\) Let \(k\in K\text{,}\) and observe that \(\varphi(k)=kK=K\text{,}\) and so \(k\in\ker(\varphi)\text{.}\) Let \(g\in\ker(\varphi)\text{,}\) meaning that \(\varphi(g)=K\text{,}\) placing \(g\in K\text{.}\) By PropositionÂ 3.38 this map is a homomorphism.
Next, suppose there exists a group \(H\) and and a homomorphism \(\varphi : G\to H\) such that \(K = \ker(\varphi)\text{.}\) Let \(g\in G\) and consider \(gKg\inv\text{.}\) Observe
\begin{equation*} \varphi(gKg\inv)=\varphi(g)\varphi(K)\varphi(g\inv)=0, \end{equation*}
given that \(\ker(\varphi)=K\text{.}\) Thus \(gKg\inv\subseteq K\) for all \(g\in G\text{,}\) making \(K\) a normal subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
