Theorem 3.43. First Isomorphism Theorem for Groups.

If \(\varphi: G \to H\) is a homomorphism of groups, then \(\operatorname{ker}(g)\mathrel{\unlhd}G\) and the map \(\overline{f}\) defined by Theorem 3.41 induces an isomorphism
\begin{equation*} \overline{\varphi}: G/\operatorname{ker}(\varphi) \xrightarrow{\cong} \operatorname{Im}(\varphi). \end{equation*}

Proof.

By Theorem 3.41, there exists a homomorphism \(\overline{f}\) such that \(\overline{f} \circ {\pi}= f\text{,}\) and its kernel consists of just the one element \(\operatorname{Ker}(f)/\operatorname{Ker}(f)\) of \(G/\operatorname{Ker}(f)\text{.}\) So \(\overline{f}\) is one-to-one, and the image of \(\overline{f}\) is the same as the image of \(f\text{.}\)
