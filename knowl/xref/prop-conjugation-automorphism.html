Proposition 1.88. Conjugation Automorphism.

Let \(G\) be a group and \(x\in G\text{.}\) Then \(\psi_g\in \operatorname{Aut}(G)\) for all \(g\in G\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(G\) be a group and \(g\in G\text{.}\) For any \(x,y\in G\text{,}\) observe
\begin{align*} \psi_g(xy) &= g(xy)g^{-1} && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-inner-automorphisms.html}{\text{Definition 1.87}}\\ &= gx(g^{-1} g)yg^{-1} \\ &= \psi_g(x) \psi_g(y) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-inner-automorphisms.html}{\text{Definition 1.87}} \end{align*}
proving that \(\psi_g\) is a homomorhism from \(G\) to itself.
I claim \(\psi_g \circ \psi_h = \psi_{gh}\) holds for any \(g, h \in G\text{.}\) Observe
\begin{align*} \psi_g(\psi_h(x)) &= \psi_g(hxh^{-1}) && \text{apply }\knowl{./knowl/xref/def-inner-automorphisms.html}{\text{Definition 1.87}} \text{ for } \psi_h \\ &= ghxh^{-1} g^{-1} && \text{apply } \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-inner-automorphisms.html}{\text{Definition 1.87}} \text{ for } \psi_g \\ &= ghx(gh)^{-1} && \knowl{./knowl/xref/thm-group-inverses.html}{\text{Theorem 1.21}}\\ &= \psi_{gh}(x) && \text{apply }\knowl{./knowl/xref/def-inner-automorphisms.html}{\text{Definition 1.87}} \text{ for } \psi_{gh} \end{align*}
Additionally, notice that \(\psi_e(x) = exe = x\) for all \(x\in G\text{,}\) and thus \(\psi_e = \operatorname{id}_G\text{.}\)
As \(\psi_g \circ \psi_h = \psi_{gh}\text{,}\) it follows \(\psi_g \circ \psi_{g^{-1}} = \operatorname{id}_G\) and \(\psi_{g^{-1}} \circ \psi_g = \operatorname{id}_G\text{.}\) This proves \(\psi_g\) has a two-sided inverse and hence is an isomorphism.

In fact, it shows that \((\psi_g)^{-1} = \psi_{g^{-1}}\text{.}\)
Thus \(\psi_g\in \operatorname{Aut}(G)\) for all \(g\in G\text{.}\)
