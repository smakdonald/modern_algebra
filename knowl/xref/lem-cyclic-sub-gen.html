Lemma 2.25. Generating Subgroups of Cyclic Groups.

Let \(G=\langle x\rangle\text{,}\) where \(x\) has finite order \(n\text{.}\) For any \(\{e\}\neq H\leq G\text{,}\) setting \(k=\min\{i \mid i\in \mathbb{Z}, i>0, x^i\in H\}\) gives that \(H=\langle x^k \rangle\text{.}\)

Proof.

Since \(H\subseteq G=\langle x\rangle\) any element of \(H\) is of the form \(x^m\) for some \(m\in \mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) By the [cross-reference to target(s) "thm-division-algorithm" missing or not unique] \(m=kq+r\) for some \(r\in \mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) \(0\leq r<k\text{.}\) Since \(x^m\) and \(x^k\) are elements of \(H\text{,}\) \(x^r=x^m(x^{k})^{-q}\in H\text{.}\) Since \(r<k\) and \(x^r\in H\text{,}\) by the minimality of \(k\) it follows that \(r\) cannot be positive and thus \(r=0\text{.}\) Therefore \(m=kq\) and we have shown that \(H\subseteq \langle x^k \rangle\text{.}\) The opposite containment follows because \(x^k\in H\) and \(\langle x^k \rangle\) is the smallest subgroup of \(G\) containing \(x^k\text{.}\) Thus \(H=\langle x^k \rangle\text{.}\)
