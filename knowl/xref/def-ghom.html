Definition 1.59. Group Homomorphism.

If \(G\) and \(H\) are groups, a homomorphism from \(G\) is \(H\) is a function \(\varphi: G \to H\) such that
\begin{equation*} \varphi(x *_G y) = \varphi(x) *_H \varphi(y)\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(*_G\) and \(*_H\) denote the multiplication rules for \(G\) and \(H\text{,}\) respectively.
