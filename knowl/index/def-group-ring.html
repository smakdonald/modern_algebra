Definition 8.39.

Let \(G = (G, \cdot)\) be a group and let \(R\) be a commutative ring with \(1\neq 0\) (often \(R\) is taken to be a field). Let \(R[G]\) be the collection of formal expressions of the form indicated below, where the \(+\) operation is assumed to be commutative:
\begin{equation*} R[G]=\left\{r_1 g_1 + r_2 g_2 + \dots + r_n g_n, n \geq 0, r_i \in R, g_i \in G\right\}. \end{equation*}
Equivalently, a typical element of \(R[G]\) can be written as \(\sum_{g \in G} r_g g\text{,}\) where \(r_g \in R\) for all \(g\) and \(r_g = 0\) for all but a finite number of \(g\)â€™s.
We can make \(R[G]\) into a ring by defining
\begin{equation*} (\sum_{g \in G} r_g g) +(\sum_{g \in G} s_g g) = \sum_{g \in G} (r_g+ s_g) g \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} (\sum_{g \in G} r_g g) \cdot (\sum_{h \in G} s_h h) = \sum_{z \in G} \sum_{(g,h), gh = z} r_g s_h z. \end{equation*}
With these definitions, \(R[G]\) is a ring, called the group ring of \(G\) with coefficients in \(R\text{.}\) It is a unital ring with identity \(1_Re_G\text{.}\) If \(R=F\) is a field then \(F[G]\) is an \(F\)-vector space.
