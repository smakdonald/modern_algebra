Definition 12.1. Linear Combination.

Let \(M\) be an \(R\)-module and \(m_1, \dots, m_n \in M\text{.}\) An \(R\)-linear combination of \(m_1, \dots, m_n\) is an element of \(M\) of the form \(r_1m_1 + \cdots + r_nm_n\) for some \(n \geq 0\) and \(r_1,\dots,r_n \in R\text{.}\)
 2 
Notice that there are no exponents here, hence the use of the term “linear”.
(If \(n = 0\text{,}\) this gives the empty sum which is interpreted to give \(0_M\text{.}\))
