Definition 6.28. Rank, Invariant Factors, Elementary Divisors.

In Theorem 6.26, the number \(r\) is the rank of \(G\text{,}\) the \(p_i^{a_{i,k}}\) are the elementary divisors of \(G\text{,}\) and the decomposition of \(G\) in parts (1-2) is called the elementary divisor decomposition of \(G\text{.}\) The decomposition in part (1) is also called a primary decomposition.
In Theorem 6.26, the number \(r\) is the rank of \(G\text{,}\) the numbers \(n_1,\ldots,n_t\) are the invariant factors of \(G\text{,}\) and the decomposition of \(G\) in part (1) is the invariant factor decomposition of \(G\text{.}\)
