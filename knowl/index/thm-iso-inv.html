Theorem 1.75. Isomorphism Invariants.

Let \(G\) and \(H\) be groups. If \(\varphi:G\to H\) is an isomorphism, then the following hold:
  1. \(|G|=|H|\text{.}\)
  2. \(|g|=|\varphi(g)|\) for all \(g\in G\) and \(|h|=|\varphi\inv(h)|\) for all \(h\in H\text{.}\)
  3. \(G\) is abelian if and only if \(H\) is abelian.

Proof.

Let \(\varphi:G\to H\) be an isomorphism of groups with inverse \(\varphi\inv\text{.}\)
  1. By TheoremÂ 1.71 \(\varphi\) is a bijective function between \(G\) and \(H\text{.}\) Thus \(|G|=|H|\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(g\in G\) and \(h\in H\text{,}\) and let \(m,n\) denote the orders of \(g,h\text{,}\) respectively. Thus
    \begin{align*} \varphi(g)^m &= \varphi(g^m) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/exe-grp-hom-order-split.html}{\text{Exercise 1.64}}\\ &= \varphi(e_G) && m \text{ is the order of } g\\ &= e_H && \knowl{./knowl/xref/thm-grphomom-preservations.html}{\text{Theorem 1.63}} \end{align*}
    and
    \begin{align*} \varphi\inv(h)^n &= \varphi\inv(h^n) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/exe-grp-hom-order-split.html}{\text{Exercise 1.64}}\\ &= \varphi\inv(e_H) && n \text{ is the order of } h\\ &= e_G && \knowl{./knowl/xref/thm-grphomom-preservations.html}{\text{Theorem 1.63}} \end{align*}
    Thus \(\varphi(g)^m=e_H\) and \(\varphi\inv(h)^n=e_G\text{.}\)
    I claim \(m\) and \(n\) are the smallest positive integers where this holds. Suppose by way of contradiction there exist \(m'<m\) and \(n'<n\) such that \(\varphi(g)^{m'}=e_H\) and \(\varphi\inv(h)^{n'}=e_G\text{.}\) But then
    \begin{align*} g^{m'} &= \varphi\inv \varphi(g^{m'}) \\ &= \varphi\inv (e_H) && \varphi(g)^{m'}=e_H\\ &= e_G && \knowl{./knowl/xref/thm-grphomom-preservations.html}{\text{Theorem 1.63}} \end{align*}
    and
    \begin{align*} h^{n'} &= \varphi\varphi\inv(h^{n'}) \\ &= \varphi(e_G) && \varphi\inv(h)^{n'}=e_G\\ &= e_H && \knowl{./knowl/xref/thm-grphomom-preservations.html}{\text{Theorem 1.63}} \end{align*}
    As \(m'<m\) and \(n'<n\text{,}\) this contradicts \(|g|=m\) and \(|h|=n\text{.}\)
  3. \((\Rightarrow)\) Suppose \(G\) is abelian, and let \(x,y\in H\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} xy &= \varphi\varphi\inv(xy)\\ &= \varphi\varphi\inv(x)\varphi\inv(y) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-ghom.html}{\text{Definition 1.59}} \\ &= \varphi\varphi\inv(y)\varphi\inv(x) && G \text{ abelian }, \varphi\inv(x), \varphi\inv(y)\in G, \\ &= \varphi\varphi\inv(yx) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-ghom.html}{\text{Definition 1.59}} \\ &= yx \end{align*}
    Thus every element two elements in \(H\) commute, making \(H\) abelian.
    \((\Leftarrow)\) Suppose \(H\) is abelian, and let \(x,y\in G\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} xy &= \varphi\varphi\inv(xy)\\ &= \varphi\varphi\inv(x)\varphi\inv(y) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-ghom.html}{\text{Definition 1.59}} \\ &= \varphi\varphi\inv(y)\varphi\inv(x) && H \text{ abelian }, \varphi\inv(x), \varphi\inv(y)\in H, \\ &= \varphi\varphi\inv(yx) && \knowl{./knowl/xref/def-ghom.html}{\text{Definition 1.59}} \\ &= yx \end{align*}
    Thus every element two elements in \(G\) commute, making \(G\) abelian.
